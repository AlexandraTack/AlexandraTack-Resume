I am a pastry chef with experience in making pastries, desserts, breads, and other baked goods. I have been baking since I was a child, and I would often help my mother in the kitchen. We would often prepare cakes, pies, and other desserts together, and she would constantly give me tips and tricks in order to ensure that our dessert would come out just right. It was at this time in my life when I truly discovered my love of baking.

I attended UC Riverside, and I am a Culinary Arts Major with an emphasis in Baking and Pastry Arts. During my time at UC Riverside, I studied abroad in Paris for a year in order to learn more about French pastries and desserts. While I was in France, I worked as a confectioner under Auguste Gusteau, the head chef and owner of Gusteau's, a five-star Parisian restaurant. In addition, I spent four months in Tokyo, studying the art of Japanese pastry-making. I interned at a pastry shop there for the summer, and I helped my friend run a pastry booth during a Tanabata festival.

After I graduated from UC Riverside, I worked as a patissier at Pangea, a multicultural restaurant specializing in international cuisine. There, I created several new pastry recipes, and prepared desserts and baked goods originating from all over the world. I specialized in French pastries and Japanese sweets, and I worked under Madeline Johnson, the head chef and manager of Pangea.

In my spare time, I enjoy spending hours in my kitchen, experimenting with baking. I'll create new pastry recipes and tweak old ones in order to make them even better. I post recipes on my baking blog online, which I update regularly with baking tips and recipes. I also enjoy teaching my family and friends to bake, and I'll periodically teach baking classes at the YMCA in Downtown Los Angeles.
